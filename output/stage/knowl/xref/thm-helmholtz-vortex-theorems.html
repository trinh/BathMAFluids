Theorem 6.2.1. The Helmholtz vortex theorems.

These state:
  1. Vortex lines move with the fluid. Thus they follow the material paths of the fluid particles. We can group nearby vortex lines together to form a vortex tube, as shown in FigureÂ 6.2.2 (taken from [4]), and the vortex tube moves with the flow. The surfaces of the vortex tube are vortex lines. This is a strong part of the reason why it is useful to calculate vortex lines.
