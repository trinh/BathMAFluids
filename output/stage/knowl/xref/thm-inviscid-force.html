Theorem 3.3.2. Forces in inviscid fluids.

In the case of inviscid fluids, we assume that the surface pressure exerted on (interior) volume elements is accounted solely by a pressure, \(p\text{,}\) which acts in the inward normal direction at each point, with \(F_n \bn = -p \bn\text{.}\) Consequently, the surface force, given by (3.3.4), is
\begin{equation} \iint_{\partial V} (-p) \bn \, \de{S} = \iiint_V (-\nabla p) \, \de{V}.\tag{3.3.5} \end{equation}
