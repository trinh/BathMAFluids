Solution.
We have that the volumetric flux along the pipe equals (\(A\) is a circular cross-section \(z=\mathrm{constant}\) of the pipe)
\begin{align*} Q=&\iint_Aw\,\de A\\ =&\int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^a\frac{G}{4\mu}\left(a^2-r^2\right)r\,\de r\,\de \theta\\ =&2\pi\frac{G}{4\mu}\int_0^a\left(a^2r-r^3\right)\,\de r\\ =&\frac{\pi G}{2\mu}\left[\frac{a^2r^2}{2}-\frac{r^4}{4}\right]_0^a\\ =&\frac{\pi G}{2\mu}\left(\frac{a^4}{2}-\frac{a^4}{4}\right)\\ =&\frac{\pi Ga^4}{8\mu}, \end{align*}
where \(G=\Delta p/L\) is the pressure gradient. Rearranging,
\begin{equation*} \Delta p=\frac{8\mu LQ}{\pi a^4}, \end{equation*}
as expected.
