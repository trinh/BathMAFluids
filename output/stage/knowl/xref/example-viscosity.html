Example 7.1.1. Thought experiment to measure viscosity.

We could imagine a thought experiment to measure the shear viscosity of a fluid as follows, see also FigureÂ 7.1.2. A solid block of area \(A\) floats on the surface of a thin layer of the fluid of thickness \(h\text{.}\) The block is pushed with a force \(F\) parallel to the surface of the fluid, causing it to move with a steady velocity \(U\text{.}\) The shear viscosity is given by
\begin{equation*} \mu = \frac{F h}{A U}. \end{equation*}
In principle this formula could be used to determine the viscosity of a fluid experimentally; however, in practice it is more common to use a falling ball viscometer (see ExerciseÂ 7.6.1 in the worksheet at the end of this chapter).
