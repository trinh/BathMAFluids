Worksheet Exercise 5. Piston problem.

The piston shown in the diagram below is pushed with a force \(F(t)\) into a pipe of length \(L\) and cross-sectional area \(A\) containing incompressible fluid of density \(\rho\text{.}\)
🔗
Assuming the fluid moves as a rigid body and neglecting friction and the mass of the piston, write down a differential equation governing the position \(X(t)\) of the piston at time \(t\text{.}\) You may neglect gravity.
🔗
Answer.
We assume that the fluid pressure at the open end of the pipe is atmospheric pressure. Since the fluid moves as a rigid body, the velocity field has no spatial gradients, and hence the Euler equation reduces to
\begin{equation*} \rho\pd{\bu}{t}=-\bnabla p. \end{equation*}
Solving for \(p\) gives
\begin{equation*} p(x,t) = \rho\dd{^2X}{t^2}(L-x)+p_{atm}, \end{equation*}
where \(p_{atm}\) is atmospheric pressure, and hence
\begin{equation*} \frac{F+Ap_{atm}}{A} = \rho\dd{^2X}{t^2}(L-X)+p_{atm} \quad\Rightarrow\quad \dd{^2X}{t^2} = \frac{F}{A\rho(L-X)}. \end{equation*}
