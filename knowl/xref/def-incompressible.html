Definition 3.4.1. Incompressible fluids.

A fluid is said to be incompressible if it preserves infinitessimal volumes. That is,
\begin{equation*} \DD{J}{t} \equiv 0. \end{equation*}
The following equivalence (iff) then follows:
\begin{equation} \textrm{Fluid is incompressible} \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad \nabla \cdot \bu = 0.\tag{3.4.1} \end{equation}
🔗

Proof.

This is immediate with Euler’s identity in Theorem 3.1.2.
